# Combat

## Purpose

Locks the combat philosophy across melee, ranged, mobs, and bosses.

## Combat Goals

- Melee and ranged are both valid.
- Combat complexity should increase with eras.
- Enemy design should force movement and loadout decisions.
- Progression should not collapse into one dominant weapon class.
- Combat is intended to remain demanding throughout the full campaign.
- Late-game enemies and bosses must stay threatening even after strong gear and firearms are unlocked.

## Balance Axes

- Damage uptime
- Mobility
- Ammo or resource cost
- Risk exposure
- Utility against planetary hazards

## Boss Pressure Rules

- Bosses should resist repetitive dominant strategies.
- Bosses should escalate through mechanics, arena pressure, and target-priority demands before relying on raw stat inflation.
- Counterplay must exist for both melee and ranged builds.
- Adaptation systems must punish over-reliance without making a player's entire build unusable.

## Intended Outcome

- Stronger player gear increases options, not guaranteed safety.
- Firearms expand combat solutions, but do not trivialize bosses.
- Late bosses should feel more aggressive, more reactive, and less exploitable than early bosses.

## Open Questions

- Is shield-based combat part of the intended loop?
- How punishing should ammo logistics be?
- What form should boss resistance take: armor phases, damage-type adaptation, weak-point windows, or something else?
