# Tech Tree

## Purpose

Maps progression from primitive survival through late-game planetary technology.

## Progression Model

- Progression is not divided into abstract eras.
- The main unit of progression is a conquered planet or planetary milestone.
- Each progression step must unlock new capabilities, not merely stronger numbers.
- The player should feel a permanent expansion of options after each mandatory world.

## Planetary Progression Template

- Planet name
- Core player problem
- New production capability
- Required boss unlock
- Required planetary material
- New traversal capability
- What previous friction this step solves

## Lock Types

- Environment lock
- Traversal lock
- Production lock
- Knowledge lock
- Combat lock

## Capability Families

- Survival capability
- Resource processing
- Power generation or storage
- Transport or ship operations
- Combat tools
- Suit or hazard protection
- Information or navigation systems

## Provisional Unlock Sequence

- Earth unlocks first-flight engineering and ship readiness
- Moon unlocks early oxygen infrastructure and first restored fast-return connection
- Planet 3 unlocks stronger power generation through new minerals
- Planet 4 unlocks stronger armor materials
- Planet 5 unlocks the forge or process needed to realize the armor tier fully
- Planet 6 unlocks firearms or advanced ranged combat
- Final progression steps remain open and should add more than numeric escalation

## Automation Policy

- Automation is mandatory for the highest-tier production chain.
- Early and mid progression should reward light-to-moderate automation, not constant micro-management.
- Automation unlocks should feel like relief from previous logistical pain.
