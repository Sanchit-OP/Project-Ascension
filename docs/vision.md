# Vision

## Purpose

Defines the project pillars, intended player experience, and non-negotiable design rules.

## Core Fantasy

- Start in primitive survival.
- Build toward industrial capability.
- Escape the homeworld through physical engineering.
- Conquer hostile planets through preparation rather than raw stats.

## Project Identity

- Project name: Project-Ascension
- Tone: science fantasy with ancient-civilization mystery
- Campaign shape: long-form handcrafted progression campaign
- Primary play mode: co-op first, solo viable
- Progression model: planet-by-planet conquest rather than abstract eras

## Design Pillars

- Space is progression, not fast travel.
- Each planet introduces a distinct survival problem.
- Technology solves previous friction and enables new verbs.
- Bosses unlock capability, not just power.
- Exploration must return meaningful information, materials, or unlocks.
- Oxygen is a core progression mechanic, not just a background meter.

## Non-Negotiable Rules

- First arrival to a planet requires physical travel.
- Players always enter a planet through orbit first.
- Planets cannot be skipped in the main progression route.
- Unique planetary resources gate future progression.
- Revisit loops must become faster after mastery.
- Team progression is shared at the team level.
- Combat is intended to stay demanding into late progression.
- Automation is required for top-tier production, but should reduce friction rather than create constant babysitting.
- Earth is naturally breathable; other worlds may require oxygen support systems.
- The homeworld is intentionally stripped down and cannot grant full vanilla progression freedom.
- Nether access is not part of the starting-world progression route.

## Current Scope Assumptions

- The critical path contains seven mandatory off-world progression steps beyond the starting world.
- A Moon stage exists in the middle of the campaign.
- The Moon is not an onboarding area; it is a real progression step with its own content, boss, and connected portal unlock.
- The player is gradually repairing an ancient interplanetary connection network.
- Optional side content exists, but should remain limited enough that players do not lose the critical path.

## Design Warnings

- Shared team progression must avoid turning late-joining players into spectators.
- Demanding late-game bosses should punish one-note strategies without hard-invalidating whole weapon classes.
- A long campaign needs pacing variety, or planets will blur together into repeated gear checks.

## Open Questions

- What is the exact order of the seven mandatory off-world steps?
- How much optional side content exists between mandatory planets?
- What is the final form of endgame mastery after the last mandatory planet?
